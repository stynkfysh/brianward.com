Appraisal Basics

A real estate appraisal is a professional opinion of a property's fair market value. It's an unbiased assessment conducted by a certified, licensed appraiser who analyzes the property's characteristics, location, and comparable sales to determine its value. Appraisals are used for mortgage lending, legal proceedings, tax purposes, insurance claims, and other financial transactions. The appraisal process includes a thorough physical inspection of the property, research of comparable sales in the area, and analysis of market conditions.

The timeline depends on the appraisal type and property complexity. The physical inspection typically takes 1-3 hours, depending on the property size and condition. The complete appraisal report, including detailed market research, comparable sales analysis, and professional conclusions, usually takes 3-5 business days from the inspection date. We offer expedited rush service available within 24 hours for properties requiring quick turnaround, with an additional fee of $175. Contact us to discuss your timeline needs.

Appraisal costs vary based on the type of appraisal and property value. Standard appraisals typically range from $300-$500. Specialized appraisals for estate, divorce, and bankruptcy purposes usually cost $400-$500. For high-value properties (over $500,000), costs may include a percentage of property value. Rush service adds $175 to the base appraisal fee. All estimates are fixed-fee with no hidden charges. Contact us at (888) 844-1733 or through our online form for a specific quote tailored to your property and appraisal needs.

Specialized Appraisals

A retrospective appraisal, also called a date of death appraisal, determines a property's fair market value as of a specific date in the past, typically the date of the property owner's death. This specialized valuation is essential for estate taxes, probate proceedings, inheritance calculations, and IRS requirements. Our appraisers are experienced in analyzing historical market conditions and comparable sales data from the relevant time period. These appraisals are court-approved and prepared to withstand IRS scrutiny. We help executors and heirs navigate the estate appraisal process with expertise and professionalism.

Divorce appraisals focus on fair market value for equitable property division in divorce settlements, while mortgage appraisals are used for lending purposes and reflect collateral value. Divorce appraisals are court-accepted valuations that must withstand legal scrutiny and potential cross-examination. They're more detailed, include extensive market analysis, and are specifically prepared to support property settlement negotiations and court decisions. Our divorce appraisals meet judicial standards and our appraiser is willing and qualified to testify regarding valuations and methodology in court proceedings if needed.

Yes, our certified appraiser is qualified and willing to provide expert testimony regarding appraisals in legal proceedings including divorce, bankruptcy, estate, and tax appeal cases. Our reports are prepared with litigation in mind, meeting all court standards with detailed documentation, clear valuation methodology, and professional conclusions. Our appraiser can explain valuation methods, comparable sales analysis, and market conclusions under oath, addressing questions from attorneys and judges. We have extensive experience providing credible expert testimony that stands up to legal scrutiny.

A Consumer Appraisal is a simplified valuation suitable for refinancing decisions, general property information, or when detailed documentation isn't required. It's less comprehensive and uses streamlined analysis. A Standard Appraisal is more thorough and detailed, including comprehensive property description, extensive market research, detailed comparable sales analysis, and court-acceptable documentation. Standard Appraisals are required for legal proceedings, divorce settlements, bankruptcy cases, and estate matters where detailed professional analysis is essential. We recommend Standard Appraisals for any situation requiring legal or financial scrutiny.
