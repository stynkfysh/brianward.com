Family Transaction Appraisal

Professional fair market value documentation for family property transfers and gift tax purposes

Understanding Family Transaction Appraisals

When real estate is transferred between family members, the IRS requires that the transaction be documented at fair market value. This applies whether the transfer is a gift, a below-market sale, or an intra-family transaction. Failing to properly document the fair market value can result in IRS challenges, gift tax complications, and potential penalties. A professional appraisal protects both the giver and receiver by establishing an objective, defensible value.

Many families make the mistake of simply agreeing on a property value without professional documentation. The IRS scrutinizes family property transfers more closely than arm's length transactions. If you transfer property to a family member without proper fair market value documentation, you risk triggering IRS inquiries, gift tax assessments, and complications with the beneficiary's tax basis in the property.

A professional appraisal creates a paper trail that satisfies IRS requirements and protects your position if the transaction is ever questioned. Our appraisals specifically address fair market value for gift tax reporting and family transfer purposes. They provide the objective, independent valuation that the IRS expects to see when family members transfer property to one another.

Whether you're gifting a home to a child, transferring property to a spouse, settling a family estate, or selling property to a family member at below-market rates, we provide the documentation needed to satisfy IRS requirements and avoid future complications. An appraisal at the time of transfer is far more cost-effective than dealing with IRS disputes later.

When You Need a Family Transaction Appraisal

  • Gifting Property to Family Members

    When you gift real estate to a child, parent, or other family member, documentation of fair market value is essential for gift tax purposes.

  • Below-Market Sales to Family

    If you're selling property to a family member at less than fair market value, an appraisal documents the discrepancy for tax purposes.

  • Estate Planning and Distribution

    During estate planning, establish fair market values for property that will transfer to heirs at your death.

  • Spousal Transfers

    Property transfers between spouses require fair market value documentation for certain tax and legal purposes.

  • Partnership or Entity Transfers

    When real estate transfers into or out of family partnerships, LLC memberships, or other entities, fair market value appraisals are required.

  • IRS Compliance and Documentation

    Establish a defensible, objective value for any intra-family transfer to satisfy IRS requirements and avoid future complications.

Family Transaction Appraisal Process

1. Transaction Consultation

We discuss the nature of your family transaction, the property involved, timing, and any specific documentation requirements for tax or legal purposes.

2. Property Inspection

Our appraiser conducts a thorough inspection documenting property condition, improvements, and all features relevant to fair market value.

3. Fair Market Value Analysis

We research comparable sales, market conditions, and property characteristics to establish an objective, defensible fair market value for the property as of the transfer date.

4. Appraisal Report

We prepare a detailed appraisal report that clearly documents fair market value and addresses the specific purpose of the family transaction.

5. Tax Documentation Support

We provide the documentation needed for gift tax returns, income tax basis reporting, and any other IRS filing requirements associated with the transaction.
