Date of Death Appraisal Service

Date of Death Appraisals, also known as retrospective appraisals, are a significant portion of Brian Ward Appraisal's business. Regular meetings are held with families settling estates needing valuations for tax purposes and estate administration.

The effective date of a date of death appraisal is some date in the past, usually when a person passed away. Historical appraisals range from one month to over 30 years prior. IRS-compliant appraisals are prepared at competitive rates.

Historical Market Research

Thorough analysis of market conditions, trends, and comparable sales at the effective appraisal date.

Comparable Sales Analysis

Identification and analysis of properties that sold during the appropriate time period with proper adjustments.

Economic Context

Consideration of market fluctuations and economic conditions affecting property values at the time of death.

IRS Compliance

Full compliance with IRS requirements for estate valuations and stepped-up basis calculations.

Appraisal Types and IRS Requirements

Standard appraisal fees apply to date of death appraisals. It is important to note that desktop appraisals do not meet IRS requirements except for vacant land. For residential and improved properties, a full Consumer Appraisal or Standard Appraisal is necessary to satisfy IRS guidelines and obtain credibility with tax authorities.
