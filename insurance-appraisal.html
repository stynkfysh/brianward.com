Insurance Dispute Appraisal

Professional property valuation to support insurance claims and resolve coverage disputes

Understanding Insurance Appraisals

When disputes arise between property owners and insurance companies regarding claim amounts, replacement costs, or property valuation, a professional, independent appraisal provides crucial third-party documentation. Insurance companies often use mass appraisal techniques or outdated valuation methods that may not accurately reflect your property's actual replacement cost or current market value.

Insurance appraisals serve several critical purposes. They can establish the pre-loss value of a property before damage occurred, document replacement costs for damaged structures, resolve disputes about coverage limits, and provide evidence to support negotiation with insurance adjusters. A professional appraisal from a licensed, independent appraiser carries significant weight in insurance disputes because it's based on professional standards and current market data.

The distinction between replacement cost and market value is crucial in insurance disputes. Replacement cost is what it would cost to rebuild or restore the property to its pre-loss condition. Market value is what the property would sell for in its current condition. Insurance policies typically focus on replacement cost, and an appraisal specifically documenting replacement requirements can support your claim for adequate compensation.

Whether you're facing a partial claim denial, believe your settlement offer is too low, or need documentation to support an appeal of your claim decision, our appraisals provide the professional evidence needed to advocate for fair treatment. We've helped numerous property owners resolve insurance disputes and obtain settlements that accurately reflect their property's value and damage extent.

When You Need an Insurance Appraisal

  • Claim Amount Dispute

    Your insurer's settlement offer seems lower than what your property should be worth or the damages warrant.

  • Coverage Denial

    Your insurance company denies coverage for damage you believe should be covered under your policy.

  • Total Loss Determination

    You disagree with the insurer's assessment that your property is a total loss or disagree with the total loss valuation.

  • Replacement Cost Documentation

    You need professional documentation of what it would cost to fully restore your damaged property.

  • Pre-Loss Valuation

    You need to establish what your property was worth before damage occurred, especially if photos or records are unclear.

  • Insurance Appeal Support

    You're appealing an insurance company decision and need independent professional documentation to support your position.

Insurance Appraisal Process

1. Claim Review

We review your insurance claim, settlement offer, denial letter, and any relevant documentation to understand the dispute and determine what appraisal will accomplish.

2. Property Inspection

Our appraiser conducts a thorough inspection documenting property condition, damage extent (if applicable), and all relevant characteristics for valuation.

3. Valuation Analysis

We analyze replacement cost, current market value, comparable sales, and repair cost estimates as appropriate for your specific dispute.

4. Detailed Report

We prepare a comprehensive appraisal report that clearly documents our analysis, methodology, and conclusions to support your insurance claim or dispute.

5. Expert Support

We can provide testimony or written responses to insurance company questions and support your negotiations or legal proceedings if needed.
