Date of Death and Step-Up in Basis Appraisals

Date of death appraisals are a significant part of our practice. When a property owner passes away, the IRS requires a fair market value opinion as of the date of death to establish the stepped-up tax basis for inherited real estate. We prepare IRS-compliant retrospective appraisals that meet the documentation requirements for estate tax returns, probate proceedings, and trust administration. Our retrospective appraisals can go back decades, and we have completed thousands of these assignments for estate attorneys, CPAs, executors, and trustees throughout San Diego County.

⚖

Divorce and Bankruptcy Appraisals

In divorce and marriage dissolution matters, an accurate property valuation is essential for equitable distribution. We handle both current-date and retrospective valuations, maintain strict confidentiality between parties, and offer a two-client option so both spouses can rely on the same impartial appraisal. For bankruptcy proceedings, our appraisals establish property value for the court in a format that is thorough, defensible, and straightforward. We have successfully supported thousands of bankruptcy filings across Chapter 7, Chapter 11, and Chapter 13 cases.

Appraisals Before Buying or Selling a Home

Considering a home purchase? A pre-purchase appraisal gives you an independent opinion of value before you commit, providing leverage to negotiate a fair price and potentially saving you thousands of dollars. If you are selling, a pre-sale appraisal helps you set an accurate asking price, avoid costly pricing mistakes, and reduce the risk of an appraisal surprise during escrow.
