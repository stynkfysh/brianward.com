About Perris Real Estate

Perris has carved out a distinctive market position centered on recreation and specialized industry. Lake Perris, a 2,400-acre reservoir, anchors the community's identity and provides boating, fishing, and water sports opportunities that attract recreational property buyers. The city's world-renowned skydiving scene, with multiple drop zones and enthusiast communities, creates a niche market of properties targeted to sport-aviation enthusiasts and adventure-oriented buyers.

The real estate market reflects Perris's diverse demographics. Residential properties range from $250,000 to $500,000, with waterfront and recreational properties commanding premiums. March Air Reserve Base's presence generates military family housing demand and provides employment stability. The emerging logistics and distribution center cluster creates opportunities for commercial property appreciation and industrial real estate investment. Newer master-planned residential communities add family amenities, while established neighborhoods maintain strong community character.

Investors view Perris as a value opportunity with multiple growth drivers: recreational appeal, military employment stability, and logistics corridor positioning. The community's unique identity, combined with affordability relative to coastal California, creates favorable conditions for property appreciation and diverse investment strategies.
