Brian Ward Appraisal's Housing Statistics Blog

Welcome to our Housing Statistics Blog where we share insights into the San Diego County real estate market. Our analysis covers median home prices, market trends, inventory levels, and the impact of interest rates on housing values. Check back regularly for the latest updates on the local housing market.

March 15, 2025

Spring Market Shows Strong Buyer Interest Despite Rising Rates

As we enter spring 2025, the San Diego County real estate market is showing increased activity. While mortgage interest rates have remained elevated, median home prices have stabilized in most neighborhoods. First-time homebuyers and downsizers are returning to the market as they adjust to current rate environments. Inventory levels remain steady, providing buyers with more options than we saw in late 2024.

Our appraisals continue to reflect the strong fundamentals of the local market, with properties in desirable locations maintaining or appreciating in value.

February 8, 2025

San Diego County Median Home Price Reaches $850,000 in Q1 2025

New data from early 2025 shows San Diego County's median home price settling around $850,000, up slightly from the $825,000 mark we saw at the end of 2024. North County communities like Carlsbad and Encinitas continue to command premium prices, while East County properties offer excellent value for buyers seeking affordability. The South Bay region has also seen renewed interest from investors looking for multi-family opportunities.

These price trends directly impact appraisal values and market-based analysis, which is why our appraisals incorporate the most current comparable sales data from throughout the region.

January 22, 2025

Inventory Levels Improve Across San Diego and Riverside Counties

January 2025 marked an important shift in supply-demand dynamics. Active inventory listings increased by 12% compared to January 2024, giving buyers more selection and negotiating power. This trend has been particularly pronounced in Riverside County communities like Temecula and Murrieta, where new construction activity has been robust.

Improved inventory levels tend to moderate price growth and provide more realistic market valuations. We're seeing this reflected in our appraisal assignments, where buyer negotiations are now based on stronger market data and less emotional pricing.

December 10, 2024

Year-End 2024 Market Summary: Interest Rate Environment Stabilizes

As 2024 came to a close, mortgage interest rates stabilized in the 6.5-7.0% range after several months of volatility. This stabilization has provided clarity for sellers and buyers alike. Year-over-year home price appreciation slowed to approximately 3-4% in most San Diego County markets, representing a return to more normalized growth rates after the explosive appreciation of 2020-2022.

Looking ahead to 2025, we expect the market to continue operating in a balanced state with moderate price growth, reasonable inventory levels, and sustainable interest rates. This environment is ideal for accurate appraisals based on true market conditions.

November 5, 2024

Riverside County Real Estate Shows Strong Resilience

Despite economic uncertainty, Riverside County residential properties have demonstrated remarkable resilience. Communities across the county, from Corona and Moreno Valley to Temecula and Lake Elsinore, have seen steady demand from both local buyers and investors relocating from higher-cost California regions. The median price point of approximately $475,000 in Riverside County continues to attract buyers priced out of San Diego's market.

Our appraisal team has noticed increased activity in Riverside County divorce, bankruptcy, and estate appraisals, reflecting both the region's population growth and the economic diversity of its residents.

October 18, 2024

San Diego's Coastal Premium Persists Despite Market Corrections

Even as some market segments experienced corrections, San Diego's coastal and near-coastal communities maintained strong valuations. La Jolla, Del Mar, Coronado, and Encinitas remain the crown jewels of the local market, with median prices exceeding $1.2 million. This coastal premium reflects both location desirability and limited supply. Meanwhile, inland East County communities have become increasingly attractive to value-conscious buyers, with solid appreciation potential.

Understanding these regional price dynamics is critical for accurate appraisals, and our appraisers maintain specialized knowledge of each distinct San Diego County market segment.
