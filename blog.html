Market Conditions Reports

Welcome to our Market Conditions blog where we share insights from our real estate market analyses across San Diego County and surrounding areas. Each report examines median home prices, inventory levels, days on market, and price trends based on MLS data. Check back regularly for the latest updates.

April 5, 2026

San Diego — Market Conditions Update (Vacant Land)

This market area, analyzed for a vacant land appraisal, reflects seller's market conditions with 2.2 months of inventory and a median sale price of $865,000, up 3.3% from the prior quarter. The median price per square foot of $564 dipped 2.8% quarter-over-quarter, while the 12-month linear trends show modest declines of −0.58% for sale prices and −1.89% for $/SF. Days on market rose from 14 to 20, and sales volume surged 31.8% to 87 transactions. With an SP/LP ratio holding at 100% and new listing activity up 26.6%, the market remains competitive with an anticipated marketing time of 30–60 days.

April 3, 2026

San Diego (Rancho Peñasquitos / Torrey Highlands), 92129 — Market Conditions Update

The Rancho Peñasquitos and Torrey Highlands area is firmly in seller's market territory with just 2.2 months of inventory and a median sale price of $2,270,000, up a striking 17.9% from the prior quarter. Median price per square foot rose 7.7% to $729, while days on market plunged from 32 to 17. The SP/LP ratio improved from 97% to 99%, and sales volume surged 44.4% quarter-over-quarter to 65 transactions. New listing activity climbed 41.9%, and the anticipated marketing time of 30–60 days underscores the competitive conditions in this upper-tier San Diego submarket.

April 2, 2026

Chula Vista, 91911 — Market Conditions Update

The Chula Vista market shows seller's market conditions with 1.7 months of inventory and a median sale price of $910,000, essentially flat quarter-over-quarter at −0.5%. Median price per square foot edged up 2.4% to $488, suggesting per-unit values remain firm despite flat headline prices. Days on market dropped sharply from 26 to 15, and the SP/LP ratio held at 100%. Sales volume declined 31.1% to 164 transactions on a quarterly basis, though year-over-year totals are up 17.2%, indicating sustained underlying demand in this market.

April 1, 2026

San Diego (Scripps Ranch), 92131 — Market Conditions Update

This Scripps Ranch area presents a balanced market with 4.4 months of inventory and 194 active listings. The median sale price of $585,000 rose 5.1% from the prior quarter, while the broader six-month comparison shows a −6.2% decline from a year ago. Price per square foot shows a positive 6.84% twelve-month trend at $317/SF, suggesting a shift in the mix of properties trading. New listing volume surged 48.8%, sales volume rose 24.5% to 132 transactions, and the anticipated marketing time of 60–90 days points to a market finding its equilibrium.

March 31, 2026

San Diego (Carmel Valley / Del Mar Heights), 92130 — Market Conditions Update

The Carmel Valley corridor continues to command premium pricing with a median sale price of $2,448,500, down just 1.3% from the prior quarter. With only 29 active listings and 2.7 months of supply, the market remains in seller's territory. Median price per square foot rose 3.1% to $835, and days on market tightened from 26 to 22. Sales volume fell 38.5% to 32 transactions, and the SP/LP ratio of 97.5% with a 30–60 day marketing time indicates competitive conditions for buyers in this high-demand area.
