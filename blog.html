Market Conditions Reports

Welcome to our Market Conditions blog where we share insights from our real estate market analyses across San Diego County and surrounding areas. Each report examines median home prices, inventory levels, days on market, and price trends based on MLS data. Check back regularly for the latest updates.

April 2, 2026

Chula Vista, 91911 — Market Conditions Update

The Chula Vista residential market is showing signs of a seller's market with just 1.7 months of inventory and an anticipated marketing time of 30–60 days. The median sale price for the most recent quarter stands at $910,000, down slightly from $915,000 in the prior quarter (−0.5%). Median price per square foot rose to $488, up 2.4% quarter-over-quarter, suggesting that per-unit values remain firm even as overall prices soften modestly. Sales volume declined 31.1% from the prior quarter to 164 transactions, though year-over-year totals show a 17.2% increase. Days on market dropped sharply from 26 to 15 days, indicating strong buyer demand for well-priced listings.

April 1, 2026

San Diego (Scripps Ranch / Mira Mesa), 92131 — Market Conditions Update

This east San Diego market area presents a balanced picture with 4.4 months of inventory and 194 active listings. The median sale price of $585,000 is up 5.1% from the prior quarter's $556,500, while the broader six-month comparison shows a −6.2% decline from the 12–18 month figure of $615,000. The 12-month linear price trend registers −11.36%, though price per square foot shows a positive 6.84% trend, suggesting a shift in the mix of properties selling. New listing volume surged 48.8% quarter-over-quarter, and sales volume rose 24.5% to 132 transactions, pointing to a market finding its equilibrium.

April 1, 2026

San Diego (Carmel Valley / Del Mar Heights), 92130 — Market Conditions Update

The luxury Carmel Valley corridor continues to command premium pricing with a median sale price of $2,448,500, down modestly from $2,480,000 in the prior quarter (−1.3%). With only 29 active listings and 2.7 months of supply, the market remains firmly in seller's territory. Median price per square foot rose 3.1% to $834.50. Sales volume dropped 38.5% quarter-over-quarter to 32 transactions, though year-over-year figures show a −13.4% change. Days on market tightened from 26 to 22 days, and the sale-price-to-list-price ratio held near 98%, indicating competitive conditions for buyers in this high-demand area.

April 1, 2026

San Diego (Mira Mesa), 92126 — Market Conditions Update

The Mira Mesa market recorded a median sale price of $588,000, up 4.3% from the prior quarter's $563,500. With 43 active listings and 3.2 months of supply, conditions lean toward sellers, with an anticipated marketing time of 60–90 days. Notably, days on market dropped 45.5% from 50 to 27 days, reflecting faster absorption. The 12-month linear trends show −6.81% for sale prices and −6.42% for price per square foot, suggesting broadly consistent downward movement across both metrics. Sales volume increased 17.6% to 40 transactions, while the SP/LP ratio held steady at 99%.

April 1, 2026

Alpine, 91901 — Market Conditions Update

Alpine's residential market is experiencing a contraction phase with the median sale price falling 14.4% quarter-over-quarter to $747,500 from $873,674. Despite declining prices, just 1.6 months of inventory and 22 active listings maintain seller's market conditions with a 30–60 day marketing time. The 12-month linear trends show −12.17% for sale prices and −10.71% for price per square foot. Year-over-year sales volume surged 65.1%, suggesting active buyer participation at lower price points. Days on market held relatively stable at 43 days, and the SP/LP ratio improved slightly to 98.6%.
