Market Conditions Reports

Welcome to our Market Conditions blog where we share insights from our real estate market analyses across San Diego County and surrounding areas. Each report examines median home prices, inventory levels, days on market, and price trends based on MLS data. Check back regularly for the latest updates.

April 3, 2026

San Diego (Rancho Peñasquitos / Torrey Highlands), 92129 — Market Conditions Update

The Rancho Peñasquitos / Torrey Highlands market is firmly in seller’s territory with just 2.2 months of inventory and a median sale price of $2,270,000, up a striking 17.9% from the prior quarter’s $1,925,000. Median price per square foot rose 7.7% to $729, while days on market plunged from 32 to 17. The SP/LP ratio improved from 97% to 99%, reflecting strong seller positioning. Sales volume surged 44.4% quarter-over-quarter to 65 transactions, and new listing activity climbed 41.9%. The anticipated marketing time of 30–60 days underscores the competitive conditions in this upper-tier San Diego submarket.

April 2, 2026

Chula Vista, 91911 — Market Conditions Update

The Chula Vista market shows seller’s market conditions with 1.7 months of inventory and an anticipated marketing time of 30–60 days. The median sale price of $910,000 was essentially flat quarter-over-quarter (−0.5%), while median price per square foot rose 2.4% to $488, suggesting per-unit values remain firm. Days on market dropped sharply from 26 to 15, and the SP/LP ratio held at 100%. Sales volume declined 31.1% to 164 transactions, though year-over-year totals are up 17.2%, indicating healthy underlying demand.

April 1, 2026

San Diego (Scripps Ranch), 92131 — Market Conditions Update

This Scripps Ranch market area presents a balanced picture with 4.4 months of inventory and 194 active listings. The median sale price of $585,000 rose 5.1% from the prior quarter, while the broader six-month comparison shows a −6.2% decline from the year-ago period. The 12-month linear price trend is −11.36%, though price per square foot shows a positive 6.84% trend, suggesting a shift in the mix of properties selling. New listing volume surged 48.8% and sales volume rose 24.5% to 132 transactions, pointing to a market finding equilibrium with a 60–90 day marketing time.

March 31, 2026

San Diego (Carmel Valley / Del Mar Heights), 92130 — Market Conditions Update

The Carmel Valley corridor continues to command premium pricing with a median sale price of $2,448,500, down modestly from $2,480,000 in the prior quarter (−1.3%). With only 29 active listings and 2.7 months of supply, the market remains in seller’s territory. Median price per square foot rose 3.1% to $835. Sales volume fell 38.5% to 32 transactions, and days on market tightened from 26 to 22. The SP/LP ratio of 97.5% and 30–60 day marketing time indicate competitive conditions for buyers in this high-demand area.

March 31, 2026

San Diego (Mira Mesa), 92126 — Market Conditions Update

The Mira Mesa market recorded a median sale price of $588,000, up 4.3% from the prior quarter. With 43 active listings and 3.2 months of supply, conditions lean toward sellers with an anticipated marketing time of 60–90 days. Days on market dropped 45.5% from 50 to 27, reflecting faster absorption. The 12-month linear trends show −6.8% for sale prices and −6.4% for price per square foot, indicating consistent downward movement year-over-year. Sales volume increased 17.6% to 40 transactions, while the SP/LP ratio held steady at 99%.
