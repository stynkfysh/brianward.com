Understanding Estate Appraisals

An estate appraisal is a critical component of the probate and trust administration process. When a property owner passes away, their real estate assets must be professionally valued to establish fair market value as of the date of death. This valuation serves multiple purposes including determining estate tax liability, equitable distribution among heirs, and satisfying IRS requirements.

Estate appraisals differ from standard appraisals in their purpose and timing. Rather than focusing on current market conditions and typical buyer-seller transactions, estate appraisals establish value specifically for legal and tax purposes. Our appraisers are experienced in meeting the heightened standards required by probate courts, trust documents, and the IRS.

The valuation date matters significantly in estate appraisals. We determine the fair market value as of the decedent's date of death, which can impact estate tax calculations and beneficiary distributions. Our comprehensive analysis considers all relevant market factors, property condition, and comparable sales from the valuation date.

Whether you're a trustee, executor, attorney, or beneficiary, we provide the professional documentation needed to satisfy all parties and regulatory requirements. Our reports are thorough, well-supported, and readily accepted by tax authorities and probate courts.

When You Need an Estate Appraisal

  • Probate Proceedings

    Courts require professional valuations to establish the decedent's estate value and guide the probate process.

  • Trust Administration

    Trustees need accurate property valuations to manage trust assets and distribute property to beneficiaries fairly.

  • Estate Tax Compliance

    The IRS requires documented fair market value for estate tax returns (Form 706) when the estate exceeds exemption limits.

  • Equitable Distribution Among Heirs

    When multiple beneficiaries inherit property or need compensation for unequal distributions, an appraisal ensures fairness.

  • Establishing Basis for Capital Gains

    Beneficiaries use the stepped-up basis from our appraisal to calculate capital gains taxes on future property sales.

  • Deed Transfers and Title Documentation

    Professional appraisals support the legal documentation required for transferring property from the estate to heirs.

Our Estate Appraisal Process

1. Initial Consultation

We discuss your specific situation, the property location, date of death, and purpose of the appraisal. We answer your questions about the process and timeline.

2. Property Inspection

Our licensed appraiser conducts a thorough interior and exterior inspection, documenting property condition as of the valuation date.

3. Market Analysis

We research comparable sales from the valuation date, analyzing market trends, economic conditions, and relevant factors that affected value.

4. Valuation and Report

Using the sales comparison approach and other relevant methods, we determine fair market value and prepare a comprehensive, court-ready report.

5. Delivery and Support

We deliver the final report and remain available to answer questions from attorneys, trustees, executors, or the IRS if needed.
