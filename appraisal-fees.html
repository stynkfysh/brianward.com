Appraisal Services and Fees

We offer flat-fee appraisals with no hidden charges. The prices below cover more than 90% of the properties we appraise. If your property requires additional work due to its size, complexity, or location, we'll let you know before you commit.

Single-Family Homes and Condos

  • $299 — Basic Desktop Appraisal. A value opinion based on public records and recent comparable sales. No property visit. Best for situations where a general estimate of value is all you need, such as a quick estate valuation or a second opinion.
  • $449 — Desktop Appraisal. A full written appraisal report with detailed analysis of comparable sales, market conditions, and property characteristics, all based on public records and MLS data. No property visit. This is the most common choice for estates, divorce, bankruptcy, bonds, and litigation support.
  • $575 — Drive-By Appraisal. Same analysis as the desktop appraisal, plus the appraiser drives to the property to photograph and observe its exterior condition and neighborhood. Useful when the property's condition or surroundings may have changed since public records were last updated.
  • $625 — Standard Appraisal. A full appraisal with an in-person interior and exterior inspection of the property, photographs, measurements, and detailed condition assessment. This is the most thorough option and is recommended for family transactions, pre-purchase or pre-sale decisions, insurance disputes, PMI removal, and any situation where the appraisal may be presented in court or to the IRS. Note: Date of death appraisals for IRS purposes require a standard appraisal — desktop reports do not satisfy IRS requirements for improved properties.

2-4 Unit Properties (Duplexes, Triplexes, Fourplexes)

  • $550 — Desktop Appraisal for 2-4 unit properties.
  • $725 — Standard Appraisal for 2-4 unit properties, including interior inspection of all units.

Add-On Services

  • $35 — Square Footage Measurement. We measure the home's living area using laser tools and provide a certified measurement. Charged per 1,500 square feet.
  • +30% — Rush Delivery. Expedited turnaround when available. We'll confirm whether rush delivery is possible before you commit.
  • $100 — Two-Client Fee. When the appraisal is prepared for two separate clients — for example, both spouses in a divorce — this covers the additional reporting and delivery.

Which Appraisal Do I Need?

If you're not sure, here's a simple guide. The key difference is whether someone visits your property in person.

A desktop appraisal ($299–$449) works well when the property's general condition is known, the appraisal is for internal or legal planning purposes, and no one needs to physically verify what the home looks like today. This is common for estate settlements, divorce negotiations, bankruptcy filings, bail bonds, litigation support, and second opinions on value.

A standard appraisal ($625) is the better choice when someone — a court, the IRS, a lender, or a buyer — needs to know that an appraiser personally inspected the property and verified its condition. This is typical for IRS date of death filings, family sales where fair market value must be documented, pre-purchase or pre-sale decisions, insurance disputes, and PMI removal requests.

When in doubt, call us at (888) 844-1733 and we'll help you figure out which type fits your situation. There's no charge for a quick consultation.

What You Receive

Every appraisal — desktop or standard — is prepared by the same certified appraiser and meets the same professional standards. Both types include a written report with an analysis of recent comparable sales in your area, a description of the property, an assessment of current market conditions, and a clearly explained value conclusion. The report is a professional document you can present to a court, attorney, CPA, or financial institution.

Standard appraisals additionally include interior and exterior photographs, a physical inspection of the property's condition, a certified measurement of the living area (if the add-on is selected), and observations about the property that may not appear in public records.

How Fees Are Determined

Our fees are based on three factors: the complexity of the property (unusual features, large acreage, or non-standard construction may require more research), the time involved in research and report preparation, and the intended use of the appraisal (reports prepared for court or IRS submission carry greater professional responsibility). For most properties, the flat fees listed above apply with no surprises.
