Challenge over-assessments and reduce your property taxes with professional documentation

Understanding Property Tax Appeals

If you believe your property has been over-assessed by the San Diego County Assessor, a professional appraisal can provide the documentation you need to challenge that assessment. Property tax appeals are an important right for property owners, and many assessments significantly exceed actual market value.

An appraisal specifically prepared for tax appeal purposes documents fair market value using current comparable sales data and comprehensive market analysis. This appraisal serves as evidence to present to the county assessment appeals board, demonstrating how the assessor's valuation diverges from actual market conditions.

Proposition 8 allows property owners to seek reduction in assessed value when the market value of the property has declined. If your property's assessed value exceeds its fair market value, you have the right to file an appeal. Our appraisals specifically document market decline and provide the professional evidence needed to support your appeal.

The county assessor's valuation is often based on limited data, historical information, or mass appraisal techniques. A professional appraisal provides a detailed, site-specific analysis that can clearly demonstrate over-assessment. Even small reductions in assessed value can result in significant annual tax savings.

When You Need a Tax Appeal Appraisal

  • Assessor's Valuation Seems High

    If you believe the county assessor's valuation exceeds what your property would sell for in the current market, an appeal is worth considering.

  • Recent Property Sales Show Lower Values

    If comparable properties in your area have sold for significantly less than the assessed value, this documentation supports an appeal.

  • Property Decline (Proposition 8)

    After market downturns, properties often retain inflated assessments. Document the decline and file for a reduction within the statute of limitations.

  • Property Defects or Deferred Maintenance

    If significant repairs are needed or property conditions have deteriorated, the assessed value may not reflect these issues.

  • Incorrect Property Description

    Assessor records sometimes contain errors regarding property size, improvements, or features that inflate assessed value.

  • Income-Producing Property

    Rental properties and commercial real estate often benefit from appraisals that accurately reflect income-based valuation.

Tax Appeal Appraisal Process

1. Review and Strategy

We review the assessor's record, your property details, and the appeal deadline. We discuss the likely value and the potential for a successful appeal.

2. Property Inspection

Our appraiser conducts a detailed inspection, noting property condition, improvements, and any factors the assessor may have missed or misvalued.

3. Market Analysis

We analyze recent comparable sales, market trends, and economic data to establish current fair market value and document any decline from assessed value.

4. Appeal Report

We prepare a comprehensive appraisal report specifically formatted for appeals board presentation, clearly demonstrating the gap between assessed and fair market value.

5. Appeals Board Support

We can appear at the assessment appeals board hearing or provide written testimony to support your appeal with professional analysis.

Frequently Asked Questions

Proposition 8, passed in 1978, allows property owners to seek a reduction in assessed value when the assessed value exceeds fair market value. This is commonly used during market downturns when properties lose value but assessments remain inflated. You must file within the statute of limitations (typically 4 years) from the date you first learned of the over-assessment.

Tax savings depend on the difference between assessed value and fair market value, your property tax rate, and whether you achieve a permanent reduction or temporary relief. Even a 5-10% reduction in assessed value can save hundreds of dollars annually. If your property is significantly over-assessed, savings could be thousands per year.

If denied at the county assessment appeals board, you can pursue further appeal through the State Board of Equalization within 60 days. However, most appeals at the county level are successful when supported by professional appraisal evidence. We discuss the likelihood of success before you proceed.

Appeal deadlines vary, but you typically have until July 15 of the year following the fiscal year you're appealing. For example, to appeal for the 2025-26 fiscal year, you must file by July 15, 2025. We recommend filing early and getting your appraisal prepared promptly after the new assessment notice arrives.
