March 20, 2026

San Diego Metro — Attached Housing Market Conditions Update

San Diego's attached housing segment is in a measured repricing phase with a median sale price of $757,975, essentially flat quarter-over-quarter but down 6.2% year-over-year. The 12-month linear trends show −8.9% for prices and −8.0% for $/SF at $628, confirming broad-based softening rather than a simple mix shift. Inventory stands at 3.5 months with 234 active listings, while days on market dropped sharply from 42 to 19. The SP/LP ratio held at 99%, and sales volume declined 29.1% quarterly though year-over-year activity is up 77.2%, indicating buyers remain active but at lower price points.

March 3, 2026

San Diego (North Park / Hillcrest), 92104 — Market Conditions Update

The North Park and Hillcrest corridor is a tight seller's market with just 2.0 months of inventory and a median sale price of $920,000, up 1.3% from the prior quarter. Price per square foot stands at $746 with a positive 1.95% six-month trend, indicating recent stabilization after a −4.2% twelve-month decline. Days on market dropped 32% from 25 to 17, and the SP/LP ratio held at 100%. Sales volume declined 25.2% to 160 transactions, driven primarily by limited supply rather than weakening demand, with a 30–60 day marketing time.

February 26, 2026

San Diego (Lakeside), 92040 — Market Conditions Update

The Lakeside market is in balanced territory with 4.6 months of inventory and a median sale price of $900,000, down 3.0% from the prior quarter. Price per square foot declined 7.7% to $393, the sharpest quarterly drop in the dataset, suggesting larger or higher-end homes are comprising more of the sales mix. Days on market improved from 42 to 28, and the SP/LP ratio held firm at 100%. Sales volume fell 35.1% quarterly but is essentially flat year-over-year at −0.9%, with a 60–90 day marketing time reflecting balanced supply-demand conditions.

February 13, 2026

Encinitas, 92024 — Market Conditions Update

The Encinitas coastal market is in a transitional phase with 3.1 months of inventory and a median sale price of $1,899,000, down 15.7% from the prior quarter's $2,252,500. Price per square foot remained comparatively stable at $957, declining just 1.2%, suggesting the headline drop reflects a mix shift toward smaller properties rather than uniform depreciation. Days on market improved sharply from 46 to 19, and the SP/LP ratio eased slightly from 99.5% to 99.0%. Sales volume fell 42.2% quarterly but is up 19.3% year-over-year, indicating an orderly repricing correction with the 60–90 day marketing time.

September 29, 2025

Oceanside, 92054 — Market Conditions Update

The Oceanside market is a seller's market with 2.3 months of inventory and a median sale price of $950,000, down a modest 1.9% from the prior quarter. Price per square foot declined 3.4% to $534, though the twelve-month linear trend of +3.8% remains positive. Days on market improved from 28 to 26, and the SP/LP ratio held firm at 100%. Sales volume was stable at 242 transactions (+2.1% QoQ), while year-over-year totals jumped 26.1%, reflecting strong demand with a 30–60 day marketing time.
