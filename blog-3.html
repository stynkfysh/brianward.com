Market Conditions Reports

March 24, 2026

La Jolla, 92037 — Market Conditions Update (Coastal)

La Jolla's coastal segment reflects balanced market conditions with 4.1 months of inventory and a median sale price of $3,670,375, up 0.6% from the prior quarter and 15.8% year-over-year. Median price per square foot of $1,377 was essentially flat quarter-over-quarter at +0.3%, while the six-month year-over-year comparison shows a strong +10.1% gain. Days on market eased from 46 to 42, and the SP/LP ratio improved from 97% to 99%. With 57 quarterly sales and an anticipated marketing time of 60–90 days, this premium coastal market shows steady appreciation.

March 20, 2026

San Diego Metro — Attached Housing Market Conditions Update

The broader San Diego metro attached housing market shows transitional conditions with 3.5 months of inventory and a median sale price of $757,975, essentially flat at −0.6% quarter-over-quarter. Year-over-year, the median has declined 6.2%, and the 12-month linear trends confirm softening at −8.9% for sale prices and −8.0% for $/SF at $628. Days on market dropped sharply from 42 to 19, and year-over-year sales volume is up 77.2%, suggesting buyers are active but repricing expectations downward. The anticipated marketing time of 60–90 days reflects a market beginning to tilt toward buyers on pricing.

March 3, 2026

San Diego (North Park / Hillcrest) — Market Conditions Update

This central San Diego market remains firmly in seller's territory with just 2.0 months of inventory and a median sale price of $920,000, up 1.3% from the prior quarter. Median price per square foot of $746 dipped 2.9% quarter-over-quarter, though the year-over-year comparison is +4.8%. Days on market improved from 25 to 17, and the SP/LP ratio held at 100%. Sales volume declined 25.2% to 160 transactions, likely driven by reduced supply rather than weakening demand, as new listing activity dropped 16.8% and the 30–60 day marketing time reflects continued competition.

February 26, 2026

San Diego (Lakeside / Eucalyptus Hills) — Market Conditions Update

The Lakeside and Eucalyptus Hills area presents balanced market conditions with 4.6 months of inventory and a median sale price of $900,000, down 3.0% from the prior quarter but up 2.1% year-over-year. Median price per square foot declined 7.7% to $393, indicating broader price softening. Days on market improved from 42 to 28, and the SP/LP ratio held at 100%. Sales volume dropped 35.1% to 87 transactions on a quarterly basis, though year-over-year totals are essentially flat at −0.9%, suggesting seasonal factors rather than fundamental demand weakness.

February 13, 2026

Encinitas, 92024 — Market Conditions Update

The Encinitas market shows seller's conditions with 3.1 months of inventory and a median sale price of $1,899,000, down 15.7% from the prior quarter's $2,252,500. Median price per square foot of $957 dipped just 1.2%, suggesting a compositional shift toward smaller properties. Days on market plunged from 46 to 19, and the SP/LP ratio of 99% indicates near-full-price transactions. Year-over-year sales volume is up 19.3% despite a 42.2% quarterly decline, pointing to an orderly repricing correction with sustained buyer demand and a 60–90 day marketing time.
