Market Conditions Reports

March 20, 2026

San Diego Metro — Attached Housing Market Conditions

Across the San Diego metro area, attached housing (condos, townhomes) traced a downward trajectory over the past 12 months. The median sale price of $757,975 was essentially flat quarter-over-quarter (−0.6%), but the 12-month linear trend shows a −8.92% decline. Price per square foot fell 2.4% to $628.22. With 234 active listings and 3.5 months of supply, the market is transitioning. New listing activity rose 16.1%, while days on market improved dramatically from 42 to 19 days. Year-over-year sales volume surged 77.2%, indicating buyers are active but repricing expectations downward.

March 3, 2026

San Diego (Normal Heights / University Heights), 92116 — Market Conditions Update

The vibrant Normal Heights and University Heights corridor remains a strong seller's market with just 2.0 months of inventory and 105 active listings. The median sale price of $920,000 rose 1.3% from the prior quarter's $908,500, and the 12-month linear trend is +1.30%. Median price per square foot of $745.99 dipped 2.9% quarter-over-quarter but is up 4.8% year-over-year. Days on market dropped from 25 to 17 days, and the SP/LP ratio held at 100%, meaning sellers are achieving full asking price. Sales volume declined 25.2% to 160 transactions, likely driven by limited supply rather than weakening demand.

February 26, 2026

San Diego (Tierrasanta / Allied Gardens), 92120 — Market Conditions Update

This mid-city San Diego market recorded a median sale price of $900,000, down 3.0% from $927,500 in the prior quarter. With 134 active listings and 4.6 months of supply, the market sits in balanced territory with a 60–90 day marketing time. The 12-month linear sale price trend is nearly flat at +0.38%, while the 6-month trend surged to +12.66%, heavily influenced by a February 2026 spike. Median $/SF fell 7.7% to $392.55, suggesting a compositional shift toward larger properties. Days on market improved from 42 to 28 days, and the SP/LP ratio held steady at 100%.

February 13, 2026

Encinitas, 92024 — Market Conditions Update

Encinitas experienced a notable price correction with the median sale price declining 15.7% quarter-over-quarter to $1,899,000. Despite this, the market remains in seller's territory with 3.1 months of supply and 53 active listings. The 12-month linear sale price trend shows −11.02%, while price per square foot held more stable at $957.07 (−1.2% QoQ). New listing volume dropped 39.2%, and days on market improved dramatically from 46 to 19 days. Year-over-year sales volume increased 19.3%, indicating healthy underlying demand even as prices undergo an orderly repricing correction.

September 29, 2025

Oceanside, 92054 — Market Conditions Update

As of late September 2025, the Oceanside market showed a competitive seller's environment with 2.3 months of inventory and 185 active listings. The median sale price of $950,000 was down 1.9% from the prior quarter, while the broader six-month median held essentially flat. Median price per square foot of $533.79 declined 3.4% quarter-over-quarter. Days on market improved from 28 to 26 days, and sellers continued to achieve 100% of list price. Sales volume rose 2.1% to 242 transactions, and year-over-year totals increased 26.1%, indicating robust market activity and healthy buyer demand.
