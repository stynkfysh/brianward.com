Market Conditions Reports

March 20, 2026

San Diego Metro (Attached Housing), Metro-wide — Market Conditions Update

The San Diego metro attached housing market (condos and townhomes) shows a transitional environment with 3.5 months of inventory. The median sale price of $757,975 was essentially flat versus the prior quarter (−0.6%), but the year-over-year decline of −6.2% and a 12-month linear trend of −8.92% confirm a broad-based softening. Median $/SF fell 2.4% to $628.22, with the 12-month linear trend at −8.03%. Despite price softness, days on market dropped sharply from 42 to 19, and new listing activity rose 16.1%. The market remains competitive on absorption but is beginning to tilt in favor of buyers on pricing.

March 3, 2026

San Diego (North Park / University Heights), 92104 — Market Conditions Update

North Park / University Heights is a competitive seller's market with just 2.0 months of inventory and properties selling at full asking price. The median sale price of $920,000 rose 1.3% from the prior quarter's $908,500, and the 12-month linear trend is positive at +1.30%. Median $/SF of $745.99 dipped 2.9% quarter-over-quarter but remains well above year-ago levels (+4.8%). Days on market dropped from 25 to 17, new listing volume declined 16.8%, and the market shows strengthening fundamentals heading into spring with tight supply and brisk absorption.

February 26, 2026

San Diego (Lakeside), 92040 — Market Conditions Update

The Lakeside market shows balanced conditions with 4.6 months of inventory and a median sale price of $900,000, down 3.0% from the prior quarter's $927,500. The 12-month linear price trend is nearly flat at +0.38%, though a February 2026 spike influenced the 6-month trend (+12.66%). Median $/SF declined 7.7% to $392.55, the largest quarterly drop in the dataset, suggesting a compositional shift toward larger properties. Despite the quarterly volume decline of 35.1% to 87 transactions, the year-over-year total is essentially flat (−0.9%), and the 100% SP/LP ratio confirms solid pricing discipline.

February 13, 2026

Encinitas, 92024 — Market Conditions Update

Encinitas is undergoing an orderly repricing correction with the median sale price declining 15.7% quarter-over-quarter to $1,899,000 from $2,252,500. Despite the headline price drop, median $/SF slipped only 1.2% to $957.07, indicating the decline is driven by a shift toward smaller or lower-priced properties rather than broad depreciation. With 3.1 months of inventory, conditions remain in seller's territory. Days on market plunged from 46 to 19, and year-over-year sales volume is up 19.3%, suggesting healthy underlying demand even as quarterly transaction counts moderated 42.2%.

September 29, 2025

Oceanside, 92054 — Market Conditions Update

Oceanside's residential market shows seller-favoring conditions with 2.3 months of inventory and an anticipated marketing time of 30–60 days. The median sale price of $950,000 dipped 1.9% from the prior quarter's $968,000. Median $/SF declined 3.4% to $533.79, though the 12-month linear trend remains positive at +3.79%. Sellers are achieving full asking price with a 100% SP/LP ratio, and days on market improved slightly from 28 to 26 days. New listing volume declined 12.8%, tightening supply further in this coastal North County market.
