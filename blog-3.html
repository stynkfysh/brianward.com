Market Conditions Reports

March 20, 2026

San Diego Metro — Attached Housing Market Conditions Update

The San Diego metro attached housing market is in a measured repricing phase with a median sale price of $757,975 (−0.6% quarter-over-quarter) and 3.5 months of inventory. Both median prices and price per square foot ($628, down 2.4%) are declining in tandem, with 12-month linear trends at −8.9% and −8.0% respectively, indicating broad-based softening. Days on market plunged from 42 to 19, and the SP/LP ratio held at 99%. Sales volume declined 29.1% quarterly but is up 77.2% year-over-year. Buyers remain active but are transacting at lower price points.

March 3, 2026

San Diego (North Park / University Heights), 92104 — Market Conditions Update

This central San Diego market is a supply-constrained seller’s market with just 2.0 months of inventory and a 30–60 day marketing time. The median sale price of $920,000 rose 1.3% quarter-over-quarter, while median price per square foot of $746 dipped 2.9%, reflecting compositional shifts in the sales mix. Days on market improved sharply from 25 to 17, and the SP/LP ratio held at 100%. Sales volume declined 25.2% to 160 transactions, driven by reduced supply (−16.8% in new listings) rather than weakening demand.

February 26, 2026

San Diego (Lakeside), 92040 — Market Conditions Update

The Lakeside market is balanced with 4.6 months of inventory and an anticipated marketing time of 60–90 days. The median sale price of $900,000 fell 3.0% from the prior quarter, while median price per square foot declined 7.7% to $393 — the largest quarterly drop in the dataset. The SP/LP ratio remained at 100%, indicating properly priced homes still sell at full ask. Days on market improved from 42 to 28 days. Sales volume declined 35.1% to 87 transactions, the most notable caution signal, though year-over-year volume is essentially flat.

February 13, 2026

Encinitas, 92024 — Market Conditions Update

The Encinitas coastal market is undergoing an orderly repricing with a median sale price of $1,899,000, down 15.7% from the prior quarter’s $2,252,500. Despite the price decline, median price per square foot of $957 held relatively steady (−1.2%), suggesting a compositional shift toward smaller properties. Inventory is tight at 3.1 months with 53 active listings. Days on market improved dramatically from 46 to 19 days, and the SP/LP ratio eased only slightly to 99%. Year-over-year sales volume is up 19.3%, confirming healthy underlying demand.

September 29, 2025

Oceanside, 92054 — Market Conditions Update

The Oceanside market presents seller’s market conditions with 2.3 months of inventory and a 30–60 day marketing time. The median sale price of $950,000 slipped 1.9% from the prior quarter, and median price per square foot declined 3.4% to $534. However, the SP/LP ratio held at 100% and days on market improved from 28 to 26. Sales volume of 242 transactions was up 2.1% quarterly and 26.1% year-over-year, reflecting strong demand. The 12-month $/SF trend of +3.8% confirms positive longer-term appreciation despite short-term softening.
