Market Conditions Reports

March 3, 2026

San Diego (Mid-City / Normal Heights), 92116 — Market Conditions Update

This supply-constrained seller's market shows just 2.0 months of inventory with an anticipated 30–60 day marketing time. The median sale price rose 1.3% quarter-over-quarter to $920,000, and is essentially flat year-over-year at $911,000 vs. $910,000. Price per square foot at $746 is down 2.9% from the prior quarter but up 4.8% year-over-year. With only 105 active listings and a 32% drop in days on market to 17 days, well-priced properties are selling quickly. The SP/LP ratio of 100% confirms competitive bidding conditions. Sales volume declined 25.2% to 160 transactions, largely attributable to supply constraints.

February 26, 2026

San Diego (Rancho Peñasquitos), 92129 — Market Conditions Update

This balanced market area carries 4.6 months of inventory with a median sale price of $900,000, down 3.0% from the prior quarter's $927,500. A notable divergence exists between headline prices and per-square-foot values: while overall prices are essentially flat year-over-year (+0.38%), the median $/SF declined 7.7% to $393, suggesting a compositional shift toward larger properties in the sales mix. Days on market improved from 42 to 28 days, and the SP/LP ratio held firm at 100%. Sales volume declined 35.1% quarter-over-quarter, though year-over-year totals remain essentially unchanged at −0.9%.

February 13, 2026

Encinitas, 92024 — Market Conditions Update

Encinitas is undergoing an orderly repricing with the median sale price falling 15.7% quarter-over-quarter to $1,899,000 from $2,252,500. Despite the price correction, just 3.1 months of inventory and 53 active listings maintain seller's conditions with an anticipated 60–90 day marketing time. Per-square-foot values have been more resilient, with the median $/SF of $957 declining only 1.2% quarter-over-quarter. Days on market dropped sharply from 46 to 19 days, indicating continued strong absorption. Year-over-year sales volume is up 19.3%, confirming that demand remains intact even as the market adjusts to lower price levels.

September 29, 2025

Oceanside, 92054 — Market Conditions Update

Oceanside's residential market reflects strong seller's conditions with 2.3 months of inventory and a 30–60 day marketing time. The median sale price of $950,000 dipped 1.9% from $968,000 in the prior quarter, while the broader six-month comparison shows essentially flat pricing (−0.5%). A divergence between the 12-month sale price trend (−3.84%) and $/SF trend (+3.79%) suggests a compositional mix shift toward smaller or more affordable properties. The SP/LP ratio stands at 100% with days on market at 26, confirming well-priced listings continue to attract competive offers. Year-over-year sales volume increased 26.1%.

June 26, 2025

Fallbrook, 92028 — Market Conditions Update

Fallbrook's residential market presents a balanced picture with 4.2 months of inventory and steady appreciation. The median sale price of $952,375 is essentially unchanged from the prior quarter (−0.3%), and up 1.3% year-over-year. Price per square foot rose 5.4% to $442, with a positive 12-month linear trend of +5.00%. Sales volume increased 17.2% to 143 transactions, indicating healthy buyer demand. Days on market rose to 50 from 31, reflecting seasonal inventory increases, while the SP/LP ratio improved to 100%. Overall, Fallbrook is characterized by stable appreciation with orderly transaction conditions.
