Frequently Asked Questions

What type of appraisal do I need for bankruptcy? +

For bankruptcy proceedings, you typically need a Standard Appraisal or Consumer Appraisal that includes a full interior and exterior inspection. These provide the detailed analysis and credible comparable properties that bankruptcy courts require. The exact requirements may depend on your specific bankruptcy case and court jurisdiction.

How long does a bankruptcy appraisal take? +

Standard bankruptcy appraisals typically take 5-7 business days from the time we inspect the property. We understand that bankruptcy cases often operate on tight timelines, and we prioritize getting your appraisal report completed promptly while maintaining the high quality and detail courts expect.

Are bankruptcy appraisals different from mortgage appraisals? +

Yes, bankruptcy appraisals serve a different purpose than mortgage appraisals. They are designed to establish accurate fair market value for legal proceedings rather than lending purposes. This means we focus on thorough documentation, credible comparable properties, and clear professional presentation suitable for court review.

Do you work with bankruptcy attorneys? +

Yes, we work regularly with bankruptcy attorneys throughout San Diego and Riverside Counties. We understand the legal requirements and can provide appraisals that meet court standards. You can have your attorney order directly, or you can contact us to arrange the appraisal yourself.
