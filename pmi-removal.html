Understanding PMI Removal

Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI) is an additional cost added to your monthly mortgage payment when you put down less than 20% on a home purchase. While PMI allows you to buy a home with a smaller down payment, it can cost hundreds of dollars every month. The good news is that once you've built up 20% equity through a combination of payments and home appreciation, you can request PMI removal.

The challenge is proving to your lender that your home is now worth enough to justify removing PMI. Many lenders require an independent appraisal to document your home's current market value and confirm that you've reached the 20% equity threshold. This is where a professional appraisal becomes a worthwhile investment.

The Homeowners Protection Act gives borrowers the right to request PMI removal once they reach 20% equity. Lenders cannot refuse a reasonable request supported by professional appraisal evidence. An appraisal from a qualified, independent appraiser like Brian Ward carries significant weight with lenders because it's conducted according to professional standards and Uniform Standards of Professional Appraisal Practice (USPAP).

If your home has appreciated since purchase, you may have more than 20% equity today even though you started with a smaller down payment. A current appraisal will document this appreciation and position you to eliminate PMI costs permanently. Even if you haven't reached exactly 20%, an appraisal showing you're close may allow you to refinance or renegotiate with your lender.

When You Need a PMI Removal Appraisal

  • You've Made Substantial Payments

  • Your Neighborhood Has Appreciated

    If home values in your area have increased, you may have reached the 20% equity threshold sooner than expected.

  • You've Made Home Improvements

    Significant renovations and upgrades increase your home's value and can push you past the 20% equity mark.

  • Your Lender Requests Appraisal

    Many lenders require a professional appraisal before approving PMI removal requests.

  • You Want to Refinance

    An appraisal can show your current equity, allowing you to refinance without PMI even if you haven't reached 20% on the original loan.

  • You're Seeking Alternative Solutions

    An appraisal demonstrating your equity can help in negotiations with your lender or when considering your options.

PMI Removal Appraisal Process

1. Initial Assessment

We discuss your situation, mortgage details, and home improvements since purchase. We explain what the appraisal will accomplish and answer your questions.

2. Property Inspection

Our appraiser conducts a thorough interior and exterior inspection, documenting all improvements and current property condition.

3. Market Analysis

We research recent comparable sales in your area to determine current fair market value and document any appreciation since your purchase.

4. Appraisal Report

We prepare a lender-ready appraisal report that clearly establishes current market value and allows your lender to calculate your equity position.

5. Lender Submission

We deliver the report directly to your lender or provide you with copies to submit. We can answer any questions the lender may have about the valuation.
