Market Conditions Reports

March 31, 2026

San Diego (Mira Mesa), 92126 — Market Conditions Update

The Mira Mesa condominium and townhome market recorded a median sale price of $588,000, up 4.3% from the prior quarter, with 3.2 months of supply leaning toward sellers. Days on market dropped 45.5% from 50 to 27, reflecting faster absorption of available inventory. The 12-month linear trends show −6.8% for sale prices and −6.4% for price per square foot at $613/SF, indicating consistent year-over-year softening. Sales volume increased 17.6% to 40 transactions, while the SP/LP ratio held steady at 99% with a 60–90 day marketing time.

March 31, 2026

Alpine, 91901 — Market Conditions Update

Alpine's residential market shows seller's market conditions with just 1.6 months of inventory, despite a median sale price decline of 14.4% to $747,500 from the prior quarter. The 12-month linear trends are notably negative at −12.2% for sale prices and −10.7% for $/SF at $463. Days on market held relatively steady at 43, and the SP/LP ratio edged up to 98.6%. Year-over-year sales volume surged 65.1%, indicating strong buyer activity at lower price points. The anticipated marketing time of 30–60 days reflects tight inventory despite the price corrections.

March 30, 2026

La Jolla, 92037 — Market Conditions Update

La Jolla's luxury market presents balanced conditions with 4.3 months of inventory and a median sale price of $3,550,000, down 1.9% from the prior quarter but up 12.4% on a year-over-year basis. Median price per square foot of $1,377 rose 2.4% quarter-over-quarter. Days on market improved from 45 to 34, while the SP/LP ratio held at 98%. Sales volume declined 30.1% to 51 transactions, and the 60–90 day anticipated marketing time reflects the premium nature of this coastal market where inventory and price discovery take longer.

March 29, 2026

San Diego (Rancho Bernardo), 92128 — Market Conditions Update

Rancho Bernardo exhibits seller's market conditions with 1.9 months of inventory and a median sale price of $1,100,000, down 6.0% from the prior quarter. Median price per square foot rose 1.8% to $643, and the 12-month linear trends are slightly positive at +0.15% for $/SF and +1.38% for sale prices. Days on market dropped from 22 to 17, and the SP/LP ratio improved from 98% to 99%. Year-over-year sales volume is up 24.8%, reflecting healthy demand in this established San Diego community with an anticipated marketing time of 30–60 days.

March 25, 2026

San Diego (Encanto / Emerald Hills) — Market Conditions Update

This San Diego submarket shows strong seller's market conditions with just 1.7 months of inventory and a median sale price of $636,625, up 0.7% from the prior quarter and 9.6% year-over-year. Median price per square foot surged 7.3% to $487, with the 12-month linear $/SF trend at an impressive +14.3%. Days on market rose from 7 to 12 but remain extremely low, and the SP/LP ratio held firm at 100%. Sales volume jumped 34.8% to 62 transactions, underscoring robust demand in this affordable entry point for San Diego homebuyers.
