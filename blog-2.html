Market Conditions Reports

March 31, 2026

Alpine, 91901 — Market Conditions Update

The Alpine market shows seller’s market conditions with just 1.6 months of inventory despite declining prices. The median sale price of $747,500 fell 14.4% from the prior quarter’s $873,674, and 12-month linear trends are negative at −12.2% for prices and −10.7% for price per square foot. However, supply remains constrained with only 22 active listings, and the SP/LP ratio improved to 98.6%. Sales volume declined 37.5% to 40 transactions, though year-over-year volume is up 65.1%. The anticipated marketing time of 30–60 days reflects the tight inventory conditions.

March 30, 2026

La Jolla, 92037 — Market Conditions Update

La Jolla’s luxury market presents balanced conditions with 4.3 months of inventory and 73 active listings. The median sale price of $3,550,000 slipped 1.9% from the prior quarter, though the six-month median of $3,590,000 is up 12.4% from the year-ago period. Median price per square foot rose 2.4% to $1,377. Days on market improved from 45 to 34, and the SP/LP ratio held at 98%. Sales volume declined 30.1% to 51 transactions. The 60–90 day marketing time and negative 6-month $/SF trend of −0.55% suggest the market is stabilizing at current price levels.

March 29, 2026

San Diego (Rancho Bernardo), 92128 — Market Conditions Update

The Rancho Bernardo area remains a seller’s market with 1.9 months of inventory and a 30–60 day marketing time. The median sale price of $1,100,000 declined 6.0% from the prior quarter, while the median price per square foot of $643 rose 1.8%, reflecting broadly consistent year-over-year movement. Days on market improved from 22 to 17, and the SP/LP ratio strengthened from 98% to 99%. Sales volume declined 17.0% to 73 transactions, though year-over-year totals are up 24.8%, indicating sustained buyer demand.

March 25, 2026

San Diego (Clairemont), 92117 — Market Conditions Update

The Clairemont market is in seller’s territory with 1.7 months of inventory and strong price appreciation. The median sale price of $636,625 was essentially flat quarter-over-quarter (+0.7%), while the six-month median of $634,750 is up 9.6% from the year-ago period. Median price per square foot surged 7.3% to $487, with the 12-month linear trend at +14.3%. Sales volume jumped 34.8% to 62 transactions. Days on market rose from 7 to 12 but remain very low, and the SP/LP ratio held at 100%, reflecting a highly competitive market.

March 24, 2026

La Jolla, 92037 — Market Conditions Update

This La Jolla market analysis shows balanced conditions with 4.1 months of inventory and 77 active listings. The median sale price of $3,670,375 was essentially stable (+0.6%), with the six-month median up 15.8% year-over-year. Median price per square foot held steady at $1,377 (+0.3%). The SP/LP ratio improved from 97% to 99%, though days on market remained elevated at 42. Sales volume declined 24.0% to 57 transactions. The 60–90 day marketing time and positive 6-month $/SF trend of +4.65% suggest strengthening fundamentals in this premium coastal market.
