Market Conditions Reports

March 31, 2026

Alpine, 91901 — Market Conditions Update

Alpine's residential market is experiencing a contraction phase with the median sale price falling 14.4% quarter-over-quarter to $747,500 from $873,674. Despite declining prices, just 1.6 months of inventory and 22 active listings maintain seller's market conditions with a 30–60 day marketing time. The 12-month linear trends show −12.17% for sale prices and −10.71% for price per square foot. Year-over-year sales volume surged 65.1%, suggesting active buyer participation at lower price points. Days on market held relatively stable at 43 days, and the SP/LP ratio improved slightly to 98.6%.

March 30, 2026

La Jolla, 92037 — Market Conditions Update

La Jolla's luxury residential market shows balanced conditions with 4.3 months of inventory and a median sale price of $3,550,000, down modestly from $3,620,000 in the prior quarter (−1.9%). Median price per square foot held firm at $1,377, up 2.4% quarter-over-quarter. The six-month median of $3,590,000 represents a solid 12.4% gain over the 12–18 month period, indicating underlying appreciation. Sales volume declined 30.1% to 51 transactions, and days on market improved from 45 to 34. With an SP/LP ratio of 98% and 73 active listings, the market remains competitive albeit with longer exposure times typical of the luxury segment.

March 29, 2026

San Diego (Rancho Bernardo), 92128 — Market Conditions Update

Rancho Bernardo presents a seller's market with just 1.9 months of supply and a median sale price of $1,100,000, down 6.0% from the prior quarter's $1,170,000. Despite the quarterly price dip, the 12-month linear trend remains slightly positive at +1.38%. Median price per square foot edged up 1.8% to $643.38, with the six-month linear trend at +3.44%. Days on market fell from 22 to 17, and the SP/LP ratio tightened from 98% to 99%, signaling firm seller positioning. Sales volume declined 17.0% to 73 transactions, though year-over-year totals climbed 24.8%.

March 25, 2026

San Diego (Encanto / Lincoln Park), 92114 — Market Conditions Update

The Encanto / Lincoln Park area shows strong seller's market conditions with 1.7 months of inventory and properties selling at full asking price (100% SP/LP ratio). The median sale price rose to $636,625, up 0.7% from the prior quarter, while the broader year-over-year comparison shows a robust 9.6% gain. Median price per square foot surged 7.3% to $486.51, with the six-month linear trend at an impressive +18.91%. Sales volume jumped 34.8% to 62 transactions. Days on market increased slightly from 7 to 12 days, though this remains an extremely fast absorption rate for this active submarket.

March 24, 2026

La Jolla / University City, 92037 — Market Conditions Update

The La Jolla / University City luxury market shows balanced conditions with 4.1 months of inventory and 77 active listings. The median sale price held steady at $3,670,375, up fractionally from $3,650,000 in the prior quarter (+0.6%). Over the broader six-month window, the median of $3,660,188 represents a striking 15.8% gain over the 12–18 month period. Median $/SF of $1,377 was essentially flat quarter-over-quarter. Days on market improved modestly from 46 to 42, while the SP/LP ratio jumped from 97% to 99%. Sales volume declined 24.0% to 57 transactions amid declining listing activity (−26.7%).
