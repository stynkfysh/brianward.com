Market Conditions Reports

March 30, 2026

La Jolla, 92037 — Market Conditions Update

La Jolla's luxury single-family market is navigating a balanced environment with 4.3 months of supply and a median sale price of $3,550,000, down 1.9% from $3,620,000 in the prior quarter. The 12-month linear price trend is positive at +4.39%, while the six-month median of $3,590,000 exceeds the year-ago figure by 12.4%. Median price per square foot stands at $1,377 with a 2.4% quarterly increase. Days on market improved from 45 to 34 days, and the SP/LP ratio held steady at 98%. Sales volume declined 30.1% to 51 transactions, reflecting tighter supply rather than weakened demand.

March 29, 2026

San Diego (Rancho Bernardo), 92128 — Market Conditions Update

Rancho Bernardo's single-family market reflects tight seller's conditions with just 1.9 months of inventory and a 30–60 day marketing time. The median sale price of $1,100,000 is down 6.0% from $1,170,000 in the prior quarter, though the 12-month linear trend remains slightly positive at +1.38%. Median price per square foot rose 1.8% to $643. Days on market dropped from 22 to 17 days, and the SP/LP ratio strengthened to 99% from 98%. Sales volume declined 17.0% quarter-over-quarter to 73 transactions, but the year-over-year total is up 24.8%, indicating sustained demand.

March 25, 2026

San Diego (Encanto), 92114 — Market Conditions Update

This San Diego neighborhood is showing strong seller's market dynamics with 1.7 months of inventory and a median sale price of $636,625, essentially flat quarter-over-quarter (+0.7%). Price per square foot surged 7.3% to $487, and the 12-month linear $/SF trend is a robust +14.30%, among the strongest in the county. Sales volume jumped 34.8% to 62 transactions, and listings are moving quickly with a median of 12 days on market. The SP/LP ratio of 100% confirms sellers are achieving full asking price. New listing volume rose 34.9%, though the year-over-year sales count is down 11.5%.

March 24, 2026

La Jolla, 92037 — Market Conditions Update

La Jolla's high-end market shows continued stability with a median sale price of $3,670,375, up 0.6% from the prior quarter. The six-month median of $3,660,188 exceeds the year-ago figure by 15.8%, and the 12-month linear trend is positive at +3.85%. With 4.1 months of supply and 77 active listings, conditions are balanced. Median price per square foot held steady at $1,377, and the SP/LP ratio strengthened from 97% to 99%. Days on market improved slightly from 46 to 42 days. Sales volume declined 24.0% to 57 transactions, though year-over-year figures remain within 4.3% of the prior period.

March 20, 2026

San Diego Metro — Attached Housing Market Conditions Update

San Diego's metro-wide attached housing market (condos and townhomes) is in a measured repricing phase with both median sale price and price per square foot declining in tandem. The median sale price stands at $757,975, essentially flat from the prior quarter (−0.6%), while the 12-month linear trend shows −8.92%. With 3.5 months of inventory and 234 active listings, conditions are balanced. Days on market dropped sharply from 42 to 19 days, and the SP/LP ratio held at 99%. Year-over-year sales volume is up 77.2%, suggesting buyers remain active but are transacting at progressively lower price points.
