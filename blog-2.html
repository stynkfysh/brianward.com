Market Conditions Reports

March 31, 2026

La Jolla, 92037 — Market Conditions Update

La Jolla's prestigious coastal market shows a median sale price of $3,550,000, down 1.9% from $3,620,000 in the prior quarter, while the broader six-month comparison reveals a strong 12.4% increase over the 12–18 month period. With 73 active listings and 4.3 months of supply, the market sits in balanced territory. Median price per square foot of $1,377 rose 2.4% quarter-over-quarter. Sales volume declined 30.1% to 51 transactions, and days on market improved from 45 to 34 days. The 12-month linear sale price trend is +4.39%, confirming the overall positive trajectory in this premium market.

March 31, 2026

San Diego (Rancho Bernardo), 92128 — Market Conditions Update

Rancho Bernardo remains a strong seller's market with just 1.9 months of inventory and 46 active listings. The median sale price of $1,100,000 is down 6.0% from $1,170,000 in the prior quarter, though year-over-year sales volume surged 24.8%. Median price per square foot rose 1.8% to $643.38, and the 12-month linear trends show modest positive movement at +1.38% for sale prices and +0.15% for $/SF. Days on market tightened from 22 to 17 days, and the SP/LP ratio improved to 99%, indicating well-priced properties are moving quickly in this family-friendly community.

March 27, 2026

San Diego (Encanto / Lincoln Park), 92114 — Market Conditions Update

The Encanto and Lincoln Park area continues to be one of the more affordable San Diego markets with a median sale price of $636,625, up 0.7% from the prior quarter. This is a seller's market with just 1.7 months of supply and 36 active listings. The 12-month linear trends are strongly positive: +2.56% for sale prices and +14.30% for price per square foot. Median $/SF jumped 7.3% to $486.51. Sales volume increased 34.8% to 62 transactions, and new listing activity rose 34.9%. Days on market remain low at 12 days, and properties are selling at 100% of list price.

March 26, 2026

La Jolla, 92037 — Market Conditions Update (Broader Area)

In a broader analysis of the La Jolla market, the median sale price reached $3,670,375, up 0.6% from $3,650,000 in the prior quarter. The six-month median of $3,660,188 represents a significant 15.8% premium over the 12–18 month figure. With 77 active listings and 4.1 months of supply, the market is balanced. Median price per square foot of $1,377 was essentially flat. Sales volume declined 24.0% to 57 transactions, while days on market improved slightly from 46 to 42 days. The SP/LP ratio improved 2.0 percentage points to 99%, and the 12-month linear sale price trend registers +3.85%.

March 20, 2026

San Diego (Rancho Peñasquitos / Torrey Highlands), 92129 — Condos & Townhomes

The condominium and townhome segment in the 92129 zip code showed notable volatility over the past 12 months, with the median sale price declining 22.2% quarter-over-quarter to $610,000 from $784,450. However, the six-month median of $717,500 remains 4.9% above the year-ago figure. Price per square foot rose 6.2% to $648.14, suggesting the price decline reflects a mix shift rather than broad value erosion. With 22 active listings and 3.9 months of supply, conditions remain competitive. Days on market dropped sharply from 47 to 16 days, indicating well-priced units are being absorbed quickly.
