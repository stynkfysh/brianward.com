Market Conditions Reports — Page 2

March 31, 2026

Alpine, 91901 — Market Conditions Update

The Alpine market shows seller's conditions with just 1.6 months of inventory but notable price softening, as the median sale price fell 14.4% quarter-over-quarter to $747,500. Price per square foot held relatively steady at $463, declining only 0.9%, suggesting the overall price decline may partly reflect a mix shift toward smaller or more affordable properties. Days on market remained stable at 43, and the SP/LP ratio improved slightly from 97.8% to 98.6%. Sales volume dropped 37.5%, though year-over-year totals are up 65.1%, and the 30–60 day marketing time reflects tight supply.

March 30, 2026

La Jolla, 92037 — Market Conditions Update

The La Jolla luxury market presents balanced conditions with 4.3 months of inventory and a median sale price of $3,550,000, down 1.9% from the prior quarter but up 12.4% year-over-year. Median price per square foot rose 2.4% to $1,377, while days on market improved from 45 to 34. The SP/LP ratio held at 98%, and sales volume declined 30.1% to 51 transactions. With 73 active listings and an anticipated marketing time of 60–90 days, the market is stabilizing and approaching a sustainable trajectory in this premier coastal community.

March 29, 2026

San Diego (Rancho Bernardo), 92128 — Market Conditions Update

Rancho Bernardo remains a seller's market with 1.9 months of inventory and a median sale price of $1,100,000, down 6.0% from the prior quarter. Price per square foot rose modestly by 1.8% to $643, and both overall prices and $/SF are moving consistently without significant compositional shifts. Days on market improved from 22 to 17, and the SP/LP ratio strengthened from 98% to 99%. Sales volume declined 17% quarter-over-quarter but is up 24.8% year-over-year, with the 30–60 day marketing time confirming competitive conditions.

March 25, 2026

San Diego (Encanto / Lincoln Park), 92114 — Market Conditions Update

This southeast San Diego market area is firmly in seller's territory with 1.7 months of inventory and a median sale price of $636,625, up 0.7% from the prior quarter and 9.6% year-over-year. Price per square foot surged 7.3% to $487, with a strong +14.3% twelve-month linear trend. The SP/LP ratio held at 100%, and sales volume jumped 34.8% to 62 transactions. Days on market increased modestly from 7 to 12 but remain exceptionally low, and the 30–60 day marketing time reflects robust demand in this affordably positioned submarket.

March 24, 2026

La Jolla, 92037 — Market Conditions Update

The broader La Jolla market shows balanced conditions with 4.1 months of inventory and a median sale price of $3,670,375, essentially flat at +0.6% from the prior quarter but up a significant 15.8% year-over-year. Price per square foot held steady at $1,377 with a positive 4.65% six-month linear trend. The SP/LP ratio improved from 97% to 99%, signaling strengthening seller positioning. Sales volume fell 24% to 57 transactions, and days on market improved from 46 to 42. The anticipated marketing time of 60–90 days reflects orderly market conditions.
