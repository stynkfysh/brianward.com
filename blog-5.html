Welcome to our Market Conditions blog where we share insights from our real estate market analyses across San Diego County and surrounding areas. Each report examines median home prices, inventory levels, days on market, and price trends based on MLS data. Check back regularly for the latest updates.

April 13, 2026

Rancho Santa Fe, 92067 — Market Conditions Update

The Rancho Santa Fe 92067 market area is a buyer's market as of the April 13, 2026 effective date. The median sale price for the most recent quarter stands at $4,750,000, down 7.2% from $5,120,000 in the prior quarter, while the median price per square foot eased 0.9% to $970.79. Supply is elevated, with 86 active listings representing 7.8 months of inventory and an anticipated marketing time of 90–120 days. Median days on market improved to 57 days from 63, though sales volume pulled back 35.3% to 33 transactions for the quarter. The sale-to-list price ratio of 94% reflects buyers negotiating meaningful discounts from ask in this luxury market segment.

Fallbrook, 92028 — Market Conditions Update

This Fallbrook 92028 update reflects the broader residential market as the best available indicator for a vacant-land subject as of the April 9, 2026 effective date, presenting a balanced market. Santee, 92071 — Vacant Land Market Conditions Update

The Santee 92071 vacant-land market is a buyer's market that is softening as of the April 9, 2026 effective date (taken from the report's file date, as the source report did not state one). Because vacant-land transactions in the immediate area are too thin to model directly, the broader Santee residential market is presented as the best available proxy: the median sale price for the most recent quarter is $867,500, down from $961,000 in the prior quarter (-9.7%), while the median price per square foot firmed to $437.14 (+4.0%), with inventory at 4.5 months and an anticipated marketing time of 60-90 days. On the land side, the most recent quarter recorded 19 closed sales at a median of $290,000 (median 3.04 acres, $1.27 per lot SF), a 94.0% sale-to-list ratio, 45 median days on market, and roughly 8.7 months of land supply. Together the metrics point to a thinly traded land market where buyers are negotiating more aggressively than in the residential segment.
