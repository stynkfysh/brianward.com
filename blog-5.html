Welcome to our Market Conditions blog where we share insights from our real estate market analyses across San Diego County and surrounding areas. May 1, 2026

San Diego, 92129 — Market Conditions Update

The San Diego 92129 (Rancho Peñasquitos) market is a strong seller's market as of the May 1, 2026 effective date. The median sale price for the most recent quarter is $1,450,000, up from $1,301,000 in the prior quarter (+11.5%), and the median price per square foot climbed to $753.34, an +11.0% quarterly gain. Inventory is exceptionally tight at 1.4 months, supporting an anticipated marketing time of 30–60 days. Median days on market shortened to just 9 from 13, and the sale-to-list ratio reached 100.0%. Quarterly sales volume rose +12.5% (63 closings versus 56), with year-over-year volume up +20.2%, underscoring robust demand.

April 29, 2026

Poway, 92064 — Vacant Land Market Conditions Update

The Poway 92064 vacant-land market is a thinly traded buyer's market that is softening as of the April 29, 2026 effective date. Restricting to the rural-residential cohort of one acre or larger that brackets the subject's 9.4-acre site, only 7 parcels closed in the trailing 12 months at a median of $225,000, versus a $388,000 median over 24 months and $475,000 over 36 months. Median sale price per acre runs $52,966 over the trailing 12 months, a softer but more stable read. Marketing time is long, with median days on market of 154 and roughly 19.5 months of inventory absorption. Declining median pricing across the 36-, 24-, and 12-month windows, lengthening marketing periods, and elevated inventory all point to continued softening for vacant land in this submarket.
